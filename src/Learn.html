Common Scam Tactics in Cambodia

üí∞

Deposit Scam

Seller asks for deposit or full payment before meeting. After receiving money, they disappear or block you.

Examples:

"Send me 50% deposit to reserve the item"

"Transfer the full amount and I'll ship tomorrow"

üéØ

Too Good to Be True

Prices far below market value for popular items like iPhones, laptops, or motorcycles.

Examples:

"iPhone 15 Pro only $200!"

"Honda motorcycle 2024 model only $500!"

‚è∞

Urgency Pressure

Creates false urgency to prevent you from thinking clearly or doing research.

Examples:

"Last one in stock, must decide now!"

"Special price today only, ends in 1 hour!"

üì∏

Fake Proof

Shows fake receipts, screenshots of other 'satisfied customers', or stolen product photos.

Examples:

"Look at all these 5-star reviews!"

"See, other people bought from me already"

Safe Shopping Practices

‚úÖ

DO These Things

  • Check seller's profile age and activity history
  • Use this platform to check if seller was reported
  • Meet in public places for local transactions
  • Inspect the item thoroughly before paying
  • Use payment methods with buyer protection
  • Ask for video calls to verify the item exists
  • Research market prices before buying
  • Trust your instincts - if it feels wrong, walk away
‚ùå

DON'T Do These Things

  • Send full payment before receiving the item
  • Send deposits to sellers you haven't met
  • Share personal information (ID, passwords)
  • Meet in secluded or unfamiliar locations
  • Use non-traceable payment methods
  • Buy items that seem too cheap to be true
  • Rush into decisions due to urgency tactics
  • Ignore red flags hoping everything will be fine
